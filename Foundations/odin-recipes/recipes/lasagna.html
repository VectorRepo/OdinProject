Lasagna

Home

Ingredients:

For the meat sauce:

  • 500g ground beef
  • 1 medium onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato pase
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • salt and black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the Béchamel Sauce

  • 50g butter
  • 50g all-purpose flour
  • 500ml milk (warmed)
  • A pinch of ground nutmeg
  • Salt and white pepper

Steps:

  1. Make the meat sauce: Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until soft. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Simmer the sauce: Stir in the tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Lower the heat and let it simmer for about 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
  3. Make the Béchamel sauce: In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk constantly for 1–2 minutes to cook out the raw flour taste. Gradually pour in the warm milk while whisking vigorously to avoid lumps. Cook, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Remove from heat.
  4. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
  5. Assemble the lasagna: Grease a baking dish. Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce at the bottom. Arrange a layer of lasagna sheets on top. Next, add a layer of meat sauce, drizzle with Béchamel sauce, and sprinkle with mozzarella cheese.
  6. Repeat the layers: Repeat the process (lasagna sheets, meat sauce, Béchamel, mozzarella) until you run out of ingredients, ensuring the top layer is just lasagna sheets covered generously with Béchamel sauce and the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake: Cover the dish with aluminum foil (make sure it doesn't touch the cheese) and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 15–20 minutes until the top is golden brown and bubbling.
  8. Rest and serve: Let the lasagna rest for about 10–15 minutes before slicing. This helps it hold its shape when served. Enjoy!
